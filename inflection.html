Inflection's goal is to build trust on the Internet, so peoplecan connect with confidence online and in person.

OUR STORY

Inflection is a trust and safety platform for the blended online and in-person connections we make today. Since 2006, we’ve organized billions of public records into solutions that verify identities and check backgrounds, while safeguarding privacy. As a Certified B Corp, we work to advance ethical information practices in all of our solutions.

our story

At first glance, ours is the classic startup story: Two college dropouts turn the glimmer of an idea into a venture-backed Internet company. Look deeper, and you’ll find something unexpected. A company that bootstrapped its way into the black before asking for funding. A company that prioritizes people as much as profit. A company that earns trust by putting privacy first and giving people control of their personal data online.

B-ING THE CHANGE

Inflection becomes a Certified B Corp, joining more than 1,800 organizations dedicated to using the power of business to solve social and environmental problems.

TRUE ME

GoodHire launches True Me to humanize background screening and reduce systemic inequality by giving people control over their own information, including the ability to add context to criminal records.

BUILDING IN TRUST

Inflection distills nine years of people data and privacy work into powerful APIs that help the on-demand economy enable trust at scale.

PRIVACY-FIRST SEARCH

The PeopleSmart redesign emphasizes simple, privacy-first search on mobile, desktop, and tablets.

YOUR DATA, YOUR CONTROL

Identity.com launches to help people trust people they meet online through a universal iD card and identity verification.

SCREENING WITH CARE

GoodHire debuts to help companies build great teams and adapt to the changing nature of work in an on-demand world.

SUPPORTING EXCEPTIONAL NONPROFITS

The Namaste Foundation opens its metaphorical doors with a $4 million gift from cofounders Matthew and Brian.

ONE FOR THE RECORDS BOOK

Ancestry.com buys the Archives.com business for $100 million. Inflection says a fond farewell to 40 Archives staffers who joined Ancestry as part of the deal.
